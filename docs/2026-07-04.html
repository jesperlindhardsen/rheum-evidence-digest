Treat-to-target strategies are feasible in elderly-onset rheumatoid arthritis given its reassuring risk-benefit profile.T3

RMD Open · PMID 42399075

Take-home: T2T-strategi er lige så gennemførlig og sikker hos ældre (>65 år) som hos yngre RA-patienter — alder alene bør ikke afholde fra aktiv behandling.

Vis abstract

To compare 2-year pharmacotherapy needs and adverse events (AEs) profiles between patients with elderly-onset rheumatoid arthritis (EORA) and young-onset RA (YORA) under a treat-to-target (T2T) strategy with a fixed medication protocol. Data from the treatment in the Rotterdam Early Arthritis cohort trial, a multicentre randomised controlled study, were analysed. Patients with RA (1987/2010 criteria) were classified as EORA (>65 years) or YORA (18-65 years). Disease Activity Score based on 44 swollen and 53 tender joint counts (DAS44) trajectories, active disease and sustained remission were assessed using mixed-effects models. Protocol-guided disease-modifying antirheumatic drug (DMARD) and glucocorticoid (GC) use, difficult-to-treat (D2T) RA prevalence and AEs were evaluated descriptively. Among 425 patients with RA, 98 (23%) had EORA (mean age 73, SD 5) and 327 (77%) had YORA (mean age 48, SD 11). Patients with EORA were more often male (50% vs 28%) and had more comorbidities (76% vs 49%). DAS44 trajectories were similar, but EORA had less often active disease and more often sustained remission. bDMARD initiation rate was lower and slower in EORA than YORA. First bDMARD survival and GC use were comparable. D2T-RA was rare and treatment adjustments due to AEs were similar. A T2T approach is feasible and effective in EORA, given its reassuring risk-benefit profile, while recognising the need for individualised management.

Beyond genotype: clustering analysis highlights clinical heterogeneity in paediatric familial Mediterranean fever.T3

RMD Open · PMID 42399078

Take-home: Klyngeanalyse af 185 børn med FMF viser stor klinisk heterogenitet selv blandt symptomatiske heterozygote — genotype alene forklarer ikke sværhedsgraden.

Vis abstract

Familial Mediterranean fever (FMF) is an autoinflammatory disease caused by mutations in the MEFV gene. Despite therapeutic advances with colchicine and interleukin-1 (IL-1) inhibitors, FMF shows marked interindividual variability, including among symptomatic heterozygotes, challenging genotype-phenotype correlations. We conducted a monocentric retrospective study including 185 paediatric patients diagnosed with FMF between 2004 and 2025 according to Eurofever criteria. Patients were stratified as genetically confirmed FMF (homozygous, compound heterozygous) or symptomatic heterozygotes. The median age at onset was 2.5 years and the median diagnostic delay was 24 months. Ninety-three (50%) patients were symptomatic heterozygotes. Compared with genetically confirmed FMF, heterozygotes had milder disease, lower colchicine doses and less frequent IL-1 inhibitor use. Cluster analysis identified five distinct subgroups, ranging from asymptomatic or mild heterozygotes without treatment to early-onset homozygous patients with severe disease requiring higher-dose colchicine and IL-1 inhibitors. Severe phenotypes were enriched in female patients with early-onset M694V homozygosity. These findings highlight the value of multidimensional approaches to better characterise disease heterogeneity.

[STING-associated vasculopathy with onset in infancy: a case report].T4

Zhonghua Jie He He Hu Xi Za Zhi · PMID 42373454

Take-home: Case af voksen-debuterende SAVI (STING-relateret vaskulopati) med interstitiel lungesygdom — genetisk udredning bør overvejes ved familiær ILD.

Vis abstract

STING-associated vasculopathy with onset in infancy (SAVI) is an autoinflammatory disease caused by mutations in the gene encoding STING. It typically presents in infancy and is mainly characterized by interstitial lung disease, skin rash, and systemic inflammation. This report presents a case of adult-onset SAVI: a 28-year-old male patient was admitted with recurrent cough, expectoration, chest tightness with shortness of breath and erythematous rashes. CT revealed bilateral diffuse fine reticular opacities with focal honeycombing. Family history revealed the patient's mother had been diagnosed with interstitial lung disease. Whole-exome sequencing identified a heterozygous STING1 mutation, establishing the diagnosis of SAVI. During one year of follow-up, the patient underwent lung transplantation and had since received long-term immunosuppressive therapy for rejection prophylaxis.
